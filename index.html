I am a postdoctoral researcher investigating how people learn the structure of the world around them (advisors: Nora Newcombe at Temple University and Russell Epstein at the University of Pennsylvania). I got my PhD from the University of Toronto, where I explored how naturalistic experience is reflected in behavior and represented in the brain across the lifespan (advisors: Morgan Barense and Morris Moscovitch). Prior to that, I completed a BSc in Psychology at University College London.
I am interested in how we navigate the world across different temporal and spatial scales, how this ability differs across people, and how we learn structured information from ongoing, everyday experience.
